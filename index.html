Frequently asked questions

What is a self-driving car?

A self-driving car is a vehicle equipped with AI technology to drive itself without human intervention.

How does a self-driving car work?

A self-driving car uses a combination of sensors, cameras, and algorithms to perceive its environment, make decisions, and control the vehicle's movements.

Are self-driving cars safe?

The safety of self-driving cars depends on the technology used and how well it has been tested. However, they generally have the potential to reduce human error, which is a major cause of road accidents.

How do self-driving cars navigate roads?

Self-driving cars use high-definition maps, GPS, and sensors to locate themselves and navigate roads. They also receive real-time updates from other self-driving cars to improve their navigation.

Who is responsible in case of an accident involving a self-driving car?

The responsibility in case of an accident involving a self-driving car depends on the specific laws and regulations of the country where the accident took place. However, in most cases, the manufacturer of the vehicle or the company operating the self-driving car service may be held responsible.

What are the benefits of self-driving cars?

The benefits of self-driving cars include reduced traffic congestion, increased mobility for people who cannot drive, and fewer road accidents due to reduced human error.
